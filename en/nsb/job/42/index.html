Language > English > New Simplified Bible > Job > Chapter 42

1JOB REPLIED TO JEHOVAH: 2»I know that you can do all things and no plan of yours can be thwarted. 3»You asked: Who is this that obscures my counsel without knowledge? Surely I spoke of things I did not understand, things too wonderful for me to know. 4»You said: Listen now, and I will speak. I will question you, and you shall answer me. 5»My ears had heard of you but now my eyes have seen you. 6»Therefore I despise myself and repent in dust and ashes.« 7»I am angry with you and your two friends, because you did not speak correctly about me, as my servant Job has. 8»So now take seven bulls and seven rams and go to my servant Job and sacrifice a burnt offering for yourselves. My servant Job will pray for you, and I will accept his prayer and not deal with you according to your folly. You did not speak correctly about me, as my servant Job has.« 9So Eliphaz the Temanite, Bildad the Shuhite and Zophar the Naamathite did what Jehovah told them. And Jehovah accepted Jobs prayer. 10After Job had prayed for his friends, Jehovah made him prosperous again and gave him twice as much as he had before. 11All his brothers and sisters and everyone who had known him before came and ate with him in his house. They comforted and consoled him over all the trouble Jehovah allowed to come upon him. Each one gave him a piece of silver and a gold ring. 12Jehovah blessed the latter part of Jobs life more than the first. He had fourteen thousand sheep, six thousand camels, a thousand yoke of oxen and a thousand donkeys. 13He also had seven sons and three daughters. 14The first daughter he named Jemimah, the second Keziah and the third Keren-Happuch. 15Nowhere in all the land were there found women as beautiful as Jobs daughters. Their father granted them an inheritance along with their brothers. 16After this Job lived a hundred and forty years. He saw his children and their children to the fourth generation. 17He died a very old man.
