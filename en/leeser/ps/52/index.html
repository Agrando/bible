Language > English > Leeser Old Testament > Psalms > Chapter 52

1To the chief musician, a Maskil of David, When Doeg the Edomite came and told Saul, and said unto him, David is come to the house of Achimelech. What vauntest thou thyself of wickedness, O mighty man? the kindness of God endureth all the time. 2Thy tongue deviseth mischiefs, like a sharpened razor, thou worker of deceit. 3Thou lovest evil more than good; falsehood more than speaking righteousness. Selah. 4Thou lovest all words of destruction, the tongue of deceit. 5Therefore God will also destroy thee for ever: he will take thee away, and pluck thee out of his tent, and root thee out of the land of life. Selah. 6And the righteous shall see it, and they will be afraid, and laugh concerning him: 7"Lo, this is the man that made not God his fortress; but trusted in the abundance of his riches, relied proudly on his mischievous wickedness." 8But I am like a green olivetree in the house of God: I trust in the kindness of God for ever and ever. 9I will thank thee for ever, because thou hast done it: and I will wait on thy name, for it is good, before thy pious ones.
