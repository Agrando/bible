Language > English > John Wycliffe Bible > Matthew > Chapter 22

1And Jhesus answeride, and spak eftsoone in parablis to hem, 2and seide, The kyngdom of heuenes is maad lijk to a kyng that made weddyngis to his sone. 3And he sente hise seruauntis for to clepe men that weren bode to the weddyngis, and thei wolden not come. 4Eftsoone he sente othere seruauntis, and seide, Seie ye to the men that ben bode to the feeste, Lo! Y haue maad redi my meete, my bolis and my volatilis ben slayn, and alle thingis ben redy; come ye to the weddyngis. 5But thei dispisiden, and wenten forth, oon in to his toun, anothir to his marchaundise. But othere helden his seruauntis, and turmentiden hem, and slowen. 6But the kyng, whanne he hadde herd, was wroth; and he sente hise oostis, and he distruyede tho manquelleris, and brente her citee. 7Thanne he seide to hise seruauntis, The weddyngis ben redi, but thei that weren clepid to the feeste, weren not worthi. 8Therfor go ye to the endis of weies, and whom euere ye fynden, clepe ye to the weddyngis. 9And hise seruauntis yeden out in to weies, and gadriden togider alle that thei founden, good and yuele; and the bridale was fulfillid with men sittynge at the mete. 10And the kyng entride, to se men sittynge at the mete; and he siye there a man not clothid with bride cloth. 11And he seide to hym, Freend, hou entridist thou hidir with out bride clothis? And he was doumbe. 12Thanne the kyng bad hise mynystris, Bynde hym bothe hondis and feet, and sende ye him in to vtmer derknessis; there schal be wepyng and grentyng of teeth. 13For many ben clepid, but fewe ben chosun. 14Thanne Farisees yeden awei, and token a counsel to take Jhesu in word. 15And thei senden to hym her disciplis, with Erodians, and seien, Maister, we witen, that thou art sothefast, and thou techist in treuthe the weie of God, and thou chargist not of ony man, for thou biholdist not the persoone of men. 16Therfor seie to vs, what it seemeth to thee. Is it leueful that tribute be youun to the emperoure, ether nay? 17And whanne Jhesus hadde knowe the wickidnesse of hem, he seide, Ypocritis, what tempten ye me? 18Schewe ye to me the prynte of the money. And thei brouyten to hym a peny. 19And Jhesus seide to hem, Whos is this ymage, and the writyng aboue? 20Thei seien to hym, The emperouris. Thanne he seide to hem, Therfor yelde ye to the emperoure tho thingis that ben the emperouris, and to God tho thingis that ben of God. 21And thei herden, and wondriden; and thei leften hym, and wenten awey. 22In that dai Saduceis, that seien there is no risyng ayen to lijf, camen to hym, and axiden him, 23and seiden, Mayster, Moises seide, if ony man is deed, not hauynge a sone, that his brother wedde his wijf, and reise seed to his brothir. 24And seuen britheren weren at vs; and the firste weddide a wijf, and is deed. And he hadde no seed, and lefte his wijf to his brother; 25also the secounde, and the thridde, til to the seuenthe. 26But the laste of alle, the woman is deed. 27Also in the risyng ayen to lijf, whos wijf of the seuene schal sche be? for alle hadden hir. 28Jhesus answeride, and seide to hem, Ye erren, `and ye knowen not scripturis, ne the vertu of God. 29For in the rysyng ayen to lijf, nether thei schulen wedde, nethir schulen be weddid; but thei ben as the aungels of God in heuene. 30And of the risyng ayen of deed men, `han ye not red, that is seid of the Lord, that seith to you, 31Y am God of Abraham, and God of Ysaac, and God of Jacob? he is not God of deede men, but of lyuynge men. 32And the puple herynge, wondriden in his techynge. 33And Fariseis herden that he hadde put silence to Saduceis, and camen togidere. 34And oon of hem, a techere of the lawe, axide Jhesu, and temptide him, 35Maistir, which is a greet maundement in the lawe? 36Jhesus seide to him, Thou schalt loue thi Lord God, of al thin herte, and in al thi soule, and in al thi mynde. 37This is the firste and the moste maundement. 38And the secounde is lijk to this; Thou schalt loue thi neiyebore as thi silf. 39In these twey maundementis hangith al the lawe and the profetis. 40And whanne the Farisees weren gederid togidere, Jhesus axide hem, 41and seide, What semeth to you of Crist, whos sone is he? Thei seien to hym, Of Dauid. 42He seith to hem, Hou thanne Dauid in spirit clepith hym Lord, 43and seith, The Lord seide to my Lord, Sitte on my riythalf, til Y putte thin enemyes a stool of thi feet? 44Thanne if Dauid clepith hym Lord, hou is he his sone? 45And no man miyte answere a word to hym, nethir ony man was hardi fro that day, to axe hym more.
