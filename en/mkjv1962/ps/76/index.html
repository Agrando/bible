1To the Chief Musician. For stringed instruments. A Psalm of Asaph. A Song. God [is] known in Judah, His name is great in Israel. 2And His abode is in Salem; and His dwelling-place in Zion. 3There He broke the fiery arrows of the bow, the shield, and the sword, and the battle. Selah. 4You [are] more glorious [and] excellent than the mountains of prey. 5The stouthearted have been stripped; they slept their sleep; and none of the men of might have found their hands. 6By Your rebuke, O God of Jacob, both the horse and the chariot have sunk into a sleep. 7You, [even] You, [are] terrifying, and who can stand when once You are angry? 8You cause judgment to be heard from Heaven; the earth feared and was still, 9when God arose to judgment, to save all the meek of the earth. Selah. 10Surely the wrath of man shall praise You; the wrath that is left, You shall bind up. 11Vow, and pay to the LORD your God; let all that are around Him bring presents to the Fearful One. 12He shall cut off the spirit in princes; [He is] feared by the kings of the earth.
