Language > English > Modern KJV 1963 > Psalms > Chapter 8

1To the Chief Musician, on the harp. A Psalm of David. O Jehovah our Lord, how excellent [is] Your name in all the earth! [You] have set Your glory above the heavens! 2Out of the mouths of babes and sucklings You have ordained strength, because of ones] vexing You, to cause the enemy and the avenger to cease. 3When I look at Your heavens, the work of Your fingers, the moon and the stars which You have established; 4what is man that You are mindful of him, and the son of man, that You visit him? 5For You have made him lack a little from God, and have crowned him with glory and honor. 6You made him rule over the works of Your hands; You have put all [things] under his feet: 7all sheep and oxen, yes, and the beasts of the field; 8the birds of the heavens, and the fish of the sea, and [all that] pass through the paths of the seas. 9O Jehovah, our Lord, how excellent [is] Your name in all the earth!
