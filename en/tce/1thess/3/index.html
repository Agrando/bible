Language > English > The Common Edition: New Testament > 1 Thessalonians > Chapter 3

1Therefore when we could endure it no longer, we thought it best to be left behind at Athens alone, 2and we sent Timothy, our brother and God's fellow worker in the gospel of Christ, to strengthen and encourage you in your faith, 3so that no one would be unsettled by these afflictions. You yourselves know that we have been destined for this. 4For indeed when we were with you, we kept telling you in advance that we were going to suffer affliction; and so it came to pass, as you know. 5For this reason, when I could endure it no longer, I also sent to find out about your faith, for fear that the tempter might have tempted you, and our labor would be in vain. 6But now that Timothy has come to us from you, and has brought us the good news of your faith and love and reported that you always remember us kindly and long to see us, just as we long to see youÂ— 7for this reason, brethren, in all our distress and affliction we were comforted about you through your faith; 8for now we really live, if you stand firm in the Lord. 9For what thanksgiving can we render to God for you in return for all the joy which we feel for your sake before our God, 10as we night and day keep praying most earnestly that we may see you face to face and supply what is lacking in your faith? 11Now may our God and Father himself, and our Lord Jesus, direct our way to you; 12and may the Lord make you increase and abound in love for one another and for all, just as we do for you, 13so that he may establish your hearts without blame in holiness before our God and Father, at the coming of our Lord Jesus with all his saints.
