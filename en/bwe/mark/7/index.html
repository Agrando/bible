1Then some Pharisees and scribes from Jerusalem came to Jesus. 2They saw some of the disciples eating food. They had not washed their hands before they ate. The Pharisees said that was wrong. 3They and all the Jews keep the laws made by men. God did not give them those laws. They do not eat until they wash their hands very well. 4When they come from the market, they do not eat until they have washed themselves. They also keep many other laws such as washing cups, pots, brass pans, and beds. 5The Pharisees and the scribes asked Jesus, Why do your disciples not keep the law our fathers made? Why do they eat with hands that are not clean? 6Jesus said to them, Isaiah spoke the truth about you people! You are not true to yourselves! He wrote about you this way: "These people respect me with their mouths. But their hearts are far from me. They do not mean it in their hearts when they worship me. Their teachings are only words of men." 7You do not keep the law of God, but you keep the laws made by people. Those laws are about washing pots and cups. You do many other things like that also. 8You break Gods law so that you can keep your own law. 9Moses said, "Respect your father and your mother. Anyone who says wrong things to his father or his mother will die." 10You say, "Tell your father or your mother that what you were going to give to them is Corban." (That means a gift to God.) 11So you stop him from doing anything for his father and mother. 12You make Gods law to mean nothing so that you can keep your own laws. You do many other things like that also. 13Then Jesus called the people to him again. He said, Listen to me, all of you, and understand what I say. 14What goes into a persons mouth does not make him dirty. But what comes out of a persons mouth makes him dirty. 15Everyone who has ears to hear, listen! 16Jesus left the people and went into a house. His disciples asked him the meaning of the story. 17He said, Do you still not understand? Do you not know this? What goes into a person from the outside cannot make a person dirty. 18It does not go into his heart, but into his stomach, and then it goes out of the body. By saying this, Jesus meant that food does not make a person dirty. 19But he said, What comes out of a person makes him dirty. 21, 20Here is what comes from the heart: wrong thoughts, all kinds of adultery, stealing, killing, wanting things that other people have, doing very wrong things, fooling people, breaking the law, jealousy, saying wrong things about people, being proud, being foolish. 21All these wrong things come from a persons heart and make him dirty. 22Then Jesus went away to the country of Tyre and Sidon. He went into a house and did not want anyone to know he was there. But he could not hide from the people. 23Right away a woman heard of him. She came and kneeled down before him. Her daughter had a bad spirit in her. 24The woman was not a Jew and had been born in the country of Phoenicia in Syria. She begged Jesus to drive the bad spirit out of her daughter. 25But Jesus said to her, We will give the children all they want to eat first. It is not right to take the childrens food and give it to the little dogs. 26She answered, Yes, Sir. But even the little dogs under the table eat food that falls from the childrens table. 27Jesus said to her, Because you have said this, you may go home. The bad spirit has gone out of your daughter. 28When she came to her house, she found that the bad spirit had left. Her daughter was lying on the bed. 29Jesus went away from Tyre and Sidon and came to the sea of Galilee. He passed through the country of the Ten Towns. 30The people brought a man to Jesus who could not hear and could not talk well. They begged Jesus to put his hand on him. 31So Jesus took him away alone. He put his fingers in the mans ears. Then he spat and touched the mans tongue. 32He looked up to heaven and sighed. He said, Ephphatha! That means, Be opened! 33Right away the mans ears were opened and his tongue was made free. He could talk well. 34Jesus said, Do not tell anyone. But the more he told them not to tell, the more they told it everywhere. 35The people were very much surprised. They said, He has done everything well. The people who could not hear can hear.
