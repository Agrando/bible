Language > English > Updated Bible Version > Matthew > Chapter 18

1Now there went with him great multitudes: and he turned, and said to them, 2He who loves father or mother more than me can't be my disciple; and he who loves son or daughter more than me can't be my disciple. 3And he who does not take his cross and follow after me, is not worthy of me. 4For which of you{+}, desiring to build a tower, does not first sit down and count the cost, whether he has [the means] to complete it? 5Lest perhaps, when he has laid a foundation, and is not able to finish, all who watch begin to mock him, 6saying, This man began to build, and was not able to finish. 7Or what king, as he goes to encounter another king in war, will not sit down first and take counsel whether he is able with ten thousand to meet him who comes against him with twenty thousand? 8Otherwise, while the other is yet a great way off, he sends an ambassador, and asks the [conditions] of peace. 9So therefore whoever he is of you{+} who does not renounce all that he has, he can't be my disciple. 10Salt therefore is good: but if the salt has lost its savor, how will it be salted? It is from then on good for nothing, but to be cast out and trodden under foot of men.
